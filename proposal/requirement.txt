Final project guidelines and rules
Group Definition
Students need to form a group of two or three members.

Project Selection
Students can choose either one of the default projects suggested by the instructors or propose their own project.

In the latter case, they first have to produce a document with clear indications of group members and specification of the project (2-3 pages).

This document should be similar to those provided by the instructors for the default projects.

The document will be submitted to the instructors by uploading it to Aula Web.

The submitted document is essential for balancing the workload of students and checking that the proposed project is on topic and original enough to differ from other submitted projects or projects of previous editions of the course (e.g.  proposals on smart agriculture may be less likely to be accepted, and proposals requiring the development of mobile apps must be coordinated with the Mobile Development course).

If the instructors think that the proposal requires some adjustment, the students will be invited to submit a revised version of their document.

Project Information
Once the project has been selected and the group is formed, students will have to publish the following information on Aula Web:

Title of the project
Names of the involved students
Estimated submission date
Technologies that will be used
Reminder: The last item is needed for checking that the minimal requirements on the project are met (see below) and for knowing if students need to access any particular resource.

Submission
The code of the project, the slides of the presentation, and any material useful for the evaluation should be shared with the instructors through GitHub.

Students will inform instructors through email, and a date for the presentation and discussion of the project will be scheduled in accordance with the instructors.

Presentation and Discussion of the Final Project
Presentations are in person, and all the students of the group are expected to collaborate. The presentation will be followed by a live demo.

Throughout the presentation and demo, students are expected to answer some technical questions from the instructors. Examples of questions include, but are not limited to, details on the code, used technologies, assessment of the application with respect to several aspects, pros and cons of the followed approach, and possible extensions and enhancements.

Final Project Submission and Presentation Deadlines
There are no fixed deadlines for the final project submission and presentation.

IMPORTANT REMARK: Dates can be scheduled in accordance with the instructors but only during the established exam breaks:

Minimal Requirements on the Project
 

At least two different edge devices, preferably of different types, should be implemented or simulated.
Edge devices do not need to be implemented with real hardware; if no hardware is provided, they should be simulated either with Node.js scripts or online simulators.
The application should provide a dashboard and the possibility to store, visualize, and download relevant data series.
The system should be based on both sensors and actuators.
At least two of the following technologies should be used: Arduino, ESP-SDK, Node.js, Node-RED, Ubidots, Influx, Grafana
 

Evaluation of the Project
The evaluation includes the developed code, the presentation, the demo, and the discussion.

The application will be evaluated according to the following criteria:

 

Originality (when applicable)
Data management
Interoperability
Implemented functionalities
Reliability
Scalability
